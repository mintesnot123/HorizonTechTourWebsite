Danakil Depression

Strategy Development

The Danakil Depression is a desert area in the Afar region of northeastern Ethiopia, north of the Great Rift Valley. This is both the hottest region on earth averaged year round and one of the most geologically active.

This is the land of “Ardi” (Ardipithecus ramidus) and “Lucy” (Australopithecus afarensis) – hominids which have been proposed as among our first putative ancestors. In June 2010, the oldest direct evidence of stone tool manufacture was found in this region and attributed to Australopithecus afarensis hominids dating back more than three million years ago...

Axum, is a city in northern Ethiopia named after the long lived Kingdom of Aksum. The 16th century Cathedral of St. Mary of Zion is built on the site of a much older church probably resembling that of Debre Damo, dating from the 4th century AD.
